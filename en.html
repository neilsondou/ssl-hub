    • About UDR Under development

    UDR (Uniform Document Retention) is a format developed by this library team, which aims to efficiently integrate documents of different formats for encryption, fast compression, and efficient sharing. By using extraction methods such as OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology, various documents (such as PDF, Word, various e-books, etc.) are converted into plain text data, and then the text data is structured and formatted to form a unified data set and stored in a large file.

    • The file in this format has the following structure
    • Header (identification, creation time, last modification time, file creator, file sequence modifier, additional information about the file)
    • Index (document ebid, title, original format, start and end position of the document in the file, author of the read document)
    • Verification (md5 value of the file, encryption algorithm related information)
    • Data (document data)
    What documents are included in this library?

    As mentioned in the introduction at the top of the page, the content we include includes but is not limited to various academic journals under the category of social sciences, Essays, monographs, novels, documentary literature, various historical materials, and popular readers. We do not have any political stance on the specific selection of which documents to include, and prefer to include more professional content. This means For example, writings about Marxism, anarchism, and philosophical categories other than political philosophy and analytical philosophy may have an empty ideological color and are separated from specific academic research or have no research value. Documents on disciplines and schools of practical significance are not included in this database. You can find them in Online Library Select submit your own document at the bottom of the page.

    The sources of documents currently in the library are: IPFS network, Open Knowledge Sharing Network (ocss), Cambridge Core journal database, original seed bookstore, zlibrary, National Philosophy and Social Sciences Documentation Center, Yuyue Library, Resilio Sync shared resources, cultural bookstore, etc.

    Our next step is to try our best to include in this library documents on various topics that are the same as those collected and preserved in various online libraries on the Internet (such as Anna’s Archive).

    What I want to say

    As “The development of Chinese social sciences, including political science, is based on the premise of being involved in the global knowledge system. The mature foreign discipline system and discourse hegemony, and the recolonization process of the knowledge world, pose a challenge to Chinese social sciences committed to independent development. Under this situation , the coexistence and collision of specialized knowledge and holistic knowledge are inevitable. The confrontation between the two, which overlaps and sharpens each other, determines the direction of the reshaping of Chinese social sciences and political science disciplines.” As mentioned in the article, the influence of foreign social science academic systems on domestic higher education and social science research began with the reform and opening up, and Deng Xiaoping’s proposal of “make-up lessons” in 1979. And now China still has a long way to go to build a complete academic system that is in line with international standards. Perhaps the obstacle is not the academic atmosphere of colleges and universities and the ability of scholars to accept new things, The main reason may be that the domestic academic environment is not open enough. Due to various pressures, we are unable to conduct in-depth and localized discussions on various foreign theories, and we cannot go too far in the depth of research. For example, the series of theoretical frameworks of "comparative political science" cannot fully explain Chinese politics, and scholars who study Chinese politics have not been able to bring a big enough impact to comparative political science from the "field" of China. ” Chinese social scientists cannot improve their own academic system based on national conditions in this environment. So far, they have only improved their own rhetoric system, which, except for propaganda, cannot play any role in academia or reality.

    Under various restrictions, whether we are scholars studying related disciplines or ordinary people, if we want to have sufficient knowledge and understanding of our country and society, and then promote the progress of civilization in our own way, whether it is to achieve political democracy, personal freedom, and social rule of law, to achieve our political civilization and modernization in a larger sense, the best way to participate in political practice and political process is to read. In most of the disciplines we call science, especially social sciences, if we want to conduct quantitative to qualitative research, thus forming a complete academic system, or just want to expand our knowledge and deepen our understanding of social operation, the cornerstone of this process is sufficient reading.

    Our vision is to establish an open and shared library of sufficient size in the future, which will be provided free of charge to anyone who needs it, in order to promote the above-mentioned progress.
